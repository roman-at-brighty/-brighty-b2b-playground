Eligibility

Has your company been economically active in the EU in the last 6 months?

What does "economically active in the EU" mean?
Your company qualifies if at least one of the following applies:
  • Registered and operating in an EU/EEA country
  • Has a business bank account with an EU-based institution
  • Invoices clients or receives payments from EU-based entities
  • Has employees or contractors based in the EU
  • Holds an active business licence issued in an EU/EEA country

Unfortunately, we can't open an account for you right now

Brighty is currently available only to companies that have been economically active in the EU within the last 6 months.

This means your company must have at least one active connection to the EU/EEA — such as being registered there, having local employees, EU clients, or an EU bank account.

If you believe this doesn't apply to your situation or you'd like to learn more, please contact our support team.

We're unable to open an account at this time

Due to regulatory requirements, Brighty is currently unable to onboard companies with US persons as ultimate beneficial owners (UBOs) or directors.

This applies to individuals who are US citizens, green card holders, or tax residents of the United States — regardless of where they currently live.

This restriction is based on FATCA compliance obligations and our current banking partnerships.

We're working to expand our eligibility criteria in the future. If you'd like to be notified when this changes, please reach out to our team.
